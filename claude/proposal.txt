1. Motivation
Large amounts of edible food are wasted daily by restaurants, grocery stores, and individuals due to overproduction, strict inventory policies, and unpredictable demand. Meanwhile, food banks and shelters experience shortages or delays in acquiring food leading to inefficiencies in distribution for an urgent humanitarian need. Existing donation processes are often slow, manual, and poorly coordinated, making it difficult to match surplus food with organizations that need it most in a timely manner. 

Food insecurity remains a significant problem in urban areas, while food waste continues to rise. Improving the speed and efficiency of food redistribution can reduce waste, lower operational costs for charities, and provide for consistent access to food for vulnerable populations. A real-time, data-driven system can significantly improve how surplus goods are identified, communicated, and allocated. 
2. Proposed Project Description 
2.1 High-Level Overview 
The proposed system, CibiNet, is a web-based platform that enables real-time coordination between food donors and food banks or shelters. Donors publish food availability while food banks can browse and claim nearby donations through a centralized system. The system supports multiple user roles, each with distinct capabilities, while maintaining a single core workflow: Donors can publish their availability, while food banks can view and claim donations. 
2.2. User Roles 
2.2.1 Individual Donors 
Individuals can donate small quantities of non-perishable food items like canned goods or packaged foods. These donations are drop-off only, meaning individuals deliver the food to a selected food bank or shelter after a claim is made. 
2.2.2 Organizational Donors (Restaurants & Grocery Stores)
Organizations are able to publish larger, bulk donations. They can manage lists of available items, quantities, and expiration windows. Donations can be marked as either pickup or drop-off, depending on the organization’s capabilities. 
2.2.3 Food Banks / Shelters
Food banks can browse available donations based on location and availability. They may claim donations, after which the donation becomes unavailable to other food banks. 
2.3 Core System Features 
Real-time donation publishing 
Atomic donation claiming to prevent double allocation 
Location-based filtering of available donations 
Automatic expiration of unclaimed donations 
Audit logs of donation claims 
Claim confirmation notifications
2.4 System Architecture 
The system is designed as a distributed web application with the following components: 
Frontend: Web interface for donors and food banks 
Backend: Handles business logic, validation, and concurrency control 
Database: Stores users, organizations, donations, and claims 
Background Workers: Handle donation expiration and cleanup 
3. Related Goals
This project aims to achieve the following objectives: 
Design and implement a distributed, role-based system 
Apply concurrency control techniques to prevent race conditions 
Build a scalable backend capable of handling real-time updates. 
Explore faulty tolerance through transactional updates and retries. 
Gain experience with system design documentation and implementation
