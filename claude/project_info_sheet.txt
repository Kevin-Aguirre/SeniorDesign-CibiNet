Project Description – Overview
CibiNet is a web-based platform designed to bridge the gap between food donors and recipients (food banks and shelters). The system will facilitate the redistributions of surplus food that would otherwise go to waste. Through CibiNet, donors publish food availability, and food banks are able to browse and claim these donations through a centralized system. 




Research, Design, Technical Issues Involved, or Addresses
Concurrency control will be implemented to ensure that two food banks cannot claim the same donation simultaneously, preventing race conditions. The application will handle real-time updates for donation availability to ensure a smooth user experience for the recipients. The design will be a multi-layered distributed application with role-based access control. Lastly, the backend of the application will be capable of handling high-frequency updates filtering donations based on geographical locations.




Goals
To reduce urban food waste and improve food security for vulnerable populations through efficient redistribution.

To democratize access to high-quality surplus from grocery stores and restaurants within the community.

To align technological efficiency with environmental sustainability.

We hope that CibiNet will foster a culture of mutual aid in the community as the application essentially lowers the barrier to entry for civic participation of food donations.
